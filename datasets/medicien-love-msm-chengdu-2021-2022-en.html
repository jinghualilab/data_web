Sexualized Drug Use Dataset among MSM in Chengdu, China (2021–2022)

Cross-sectional (Dec 2021–Feb 2022, Chengdu); TPB model; non-random sampling; 727 valid participants (86.8% response rate).

Study Introduction

The primary objective of this database is to explore the factors influencing sexualized drug use (SDU) behavior among men who have sex with men (MSM), based on the Theory of Planned Behavior (TPB) model. This cross-sectional study was conducted in Chengdu, China, from December 2021 to February 2022, employing a non-random sampling method.

Participants were recruited in collaboration with a local MSM-friendly organization, the Chengdu Tongle Health Consultation Service Center, through three primary channels: (1) MSM receiving HIV testing and counseling services at Tongle, (2) outreach surveys conducted at MSM gathering venues such as bars and bathhouses, and (3) online invitations sent via Tongle's volunteer peer network. A total of 838 MSM were approached, with 727 valid participants, yielding an 86.8% response rate.

Questionnaire & Coding

(1) General Demographic Characteristics: Age, ethnicity, place of residence, educational attainment, marital status, employment status, average monthly personal income, perceived economic situation, and sexual orientation.

(2) Sexualized Drug Use: Engagement in SDU in the past six months, drug types used, reasons for engaging in SDU, voluntary or forced use, SDU frequency, poly-drug use, time since first SDU, locations of SDU, expenditure on drugs, dosage, routes of drug acquisition, craving, perceived loss of control, severity of SDU-related problems, attempts to quit, intention to quit, and self-efficacy for cessation.

(3) Sexual Activity: Age at first same-sex sexual experience, sexual role, sexual practices in the past six months, numbers of regular, casual and commercial partners, condom use with different partner types, sexual partners with HIV-positive or unknown status, participation in group sex, history of sexually transmitted infections (STIs), HIV testing history and results, and perceived HIV infection risk.

(4) TPB-Related Measures: Behavioral attitudes toward SDU (positive and negative), subjective norms (perceived support or opposition from partners, friends, family and social networks), perceived behavioral control (ability to refuse SDU invitations and to obtain drugs), and behavioral intentions to engage in SDU in the next month and next six months.

(5) Additional Variables: Frequency and amount of alcohol consumption and alcohol-related sexual behavior, sexual sensation seeking, minority stress and internalized stigma among MSM, environmental constraints related to SDU (limited education and regulation, peer norms, and drug availability), depressive symptoms, suicidal ideation/planning/attempts, HIV test results, HIV risk perception, and SDU cessation behavior, perceived difficulty and confidence.

Publications

  1. Gan, L., Chen, X., Yang, X., et al. (2022). Prevalence of chemsex and associated factors among men who have sex with men. Chinese Journal of AIDS & STD, 28(10):1154–1158.. DOI:10.13419/j.cnki.aids.2022.10.09

    Abstract: Objective To understand the characteristics and related factors of drug-related sexual behavior (chemsex) among men who have sex with men (MSM). Methods A questionnaire survey was conducted to collect basic information, sexual behavior characteristics and chemsex experience among MSM in Chengdu. Univariate and multivariate analyses were used to explore characteristics and related factors of chemsex. Results Among 683 MSM with a mean age of 29.15 years, 44.95% reported chemsex experience; 48.32% had more than one sexual partner in the preceding 6 months, and 67.79% mainly sought partners via the internet. Multivariate analysis showed that first sex with men before age 18, having more than one sexual partner in the past 6 months, seeking partners online, having partners who were HIV-positive or of unknown status, exposure to positive information about chemsex, and receiving invitations or information about chemsex and drug-selling channels were all positively associated with chemsex. Conclusions Having multiple partners and not paying attention to partners' HIV status were highly correlated with chemsex. Early sexual debut, online dating and exposure to online promotion information may further facilitate chemsex, highlighting the need for strengthened sexual health education for adolescents and tighter supervision of online information.

  2. Chen, X., Cai, Y., Gan, L., et al. (2023). Cluster analysis of sexualized drug use among men who have sex with men and its impact on high risk sexual behaviors in Chengdu. Chinese Journal of Epidemiology, 44(2):268‚Äì275.. DOI:10.3760/cma.j.cn112338-20220722-00651

    Abstract: Objective To understand characteristics of sexualized drug use (SDU) among MSM in Chengdu, classify behavioral patterns using a two-step cluster algorithm, and explore associations between SDU patterns and HIV-related high-risk sexual behaviors. Methods A cross-sectional survey was conducted among MSM from December 2021 to February 2022. Data on sociodemographics, SDU characteristics, sexual behaviors, STD history and HIV infection were collected. Three SDU behavioral clusters were identified. Significant between-cluster differences were observed in monthly income, drug types, modes of use, SDU frequency, polydrug use, multiple partners, inconsistent condom use and group sex. Compared with the first cluster, MSM in the third cluster were more likely not to use condoms consistently (adjusted OR = 2.22); MSM in the second and third clusters were more likely to engage in group sex (adjusted OR = 8.78 and 2.82, respectively). Conclusions More than one-third of MSM reported SDU, and different SDU clusters were associated with distinct high-risk sexual behavior profiles. MSM with higher SDU frequency, larger drug dosage and polydrug use were more likely to engage in group sex and inconsistent condom use, increasing their HIV/STD risk and underscoring the need to strengthen SDU surveillance and interventions.

  3. Xu, J., Lin, H., Chen, X., et al. (2024). Intention to quit Rush among men who have sex with men and its influencing factors based on the theory of planned behavior. Chinese Journal of AIDS & STD, 30(3):282–288.. DOI:10.13419/j.cnki.aids.2024.03.11

    Abstract: Objective To explore factors influencing the intention to quit Rush among the chemsex subgroup of MSM based on the Theory of Planned Behavior, in order to identify key populations for intervention. Methods From October 2021 to January 2022, an on-site questionnaire survey collected data on sociodemographic characteristics, sexual behaviors, Rush use and cessation intention, and TPB-related constructs. Among 184 MSM who had used Rush in the past 6 months, 31.5% planned to quit in the next 6 months. In multivariate models, having a regular partner, polydrug use and greater amounts of Rush use were negatively associated with intention to quit. Among TPB constructs, only subjective norms were significantly associated with quitting intention; other TPB elements showed no significant associations. Conclusions The intention to quit Rush among MSM with chemsex behavior was low. Peer support environments and positive attitudes toward Rush use were associated with lower intention to quit, indicating that interventions should target social norms and peer contexts.
